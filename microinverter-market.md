<p><p>The microinverter market consists of two primary types: standalone and on-grid systems. Standalone microinverters operate independently, allowing for off-grid solar energy generation, suitable for remote areas where grid access is limited. Conversely, on-grid microinverters are designed to connect with the electrical grid, enabling excess energy production to be sold back to utility companies. Both market types cater to different consumer needs, with standalone systems emphasizing energy independence and on-grid systems focusing on efficiency and economic benefits through grid interaction.</p></p>
