<p><p>Malleable iron castings are manufactured using two primary molding techniques: vertical molding and horizontal molding. Vertical molding involves the arrangement of molds in a vertical orientation, facilitating efficient work and reduced space requirements. This method enhances production speed and is ideal for high-volume components. In contrast, horizontal molding places molds flat, allowing for easier accessibility and larger cast sizes. Both methods cater to different production needs, influencing factors such as surface finish, dimensional accuracy, and operational efficiency in malleable iron casting.</p></p>
