<p><p>The osteoporosis therapeutics market comprises several key types, each targeting bone density and strength. Bisphosphonates are the most commonly prescribed, inhibiting bone resorption. Hormonal treatments like estrogens help maintain bone mass. Strontium ranelate offers a dual-action approach to bone formation and resorption. Raloxifene, a selective estrogen receptor modulator, reduces fracture risk. Denosumab, a monoclonal antibody, works by inhibiting osteoclast formation. Other therapies may include calcitonin and nutritional supplements, expanding options for patient care and management.</p></p>
