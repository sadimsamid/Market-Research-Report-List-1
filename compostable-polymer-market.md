<p><p>The compostable polymer market consists of several key types. Starch-based plastics utilize renewable starch as a base, providing biodegradable options. Polylactic Acid (PLA) is derived from corn starch or sugarcane, known for its versatility. Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA) are produced by microbial fermentation, offering biodegradability in various environments. Polyesters like PBS, PBAT, and PCL feature different properties suitable for applications in packaging and textiles. Lastly, cellulose derivatives, derived from plant materials, serve as eco-friendly alternatives in various industrial applications.</p></p>
